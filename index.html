Learn to Dance

Starting dance can be daunting… We all know because we have all been beginners once! This is why we want to offer our newcomers as many options as possible so that you can find what suits you best.

Have a look through our classes aimed at beginners to see which one excites you most. You can join more than one too!

Competitive Beginners Team

If you ever felt in awe of the grace of the Waltz or the sharpness of the Cha-cha-cha, if you ever felt inspired by the stars on Strictly Come Dancing, if the rhythm in songs doesn't let you stand still, or if you simply want to have fun, meet great people and be part of a team - this is the class for you! Come and join one of the most popular societies at Imperial - our outstanding teachers will teach you everything from the very beginning!

Salsa Beginners

This is a great option if you have never danced before and want to do something fun, distracting, and meet loads of new people! Salsa is a very social dance and there are a lot of opportunities to show off your moves at the socials that we organise throughout the year, and also outside university in the many salsa bars and clubs around London! For more info, have a look at the Salsa section.

Casual Ballroom for Beginners

Competitions are not for everyone - sometimes you simply want to learn how to dance beautifully, do something relaxing for a break of your daily schedule, and get to know other people without having to commit as much. If that’s the case, come and try out our Casual Ballroom and Latin classes! One of the major aims of these lessons is to teach you some of the most important dance skills: leading and following! The goal is to be able to dance with people you may not have danced with before, feel confident and make it look awesome!

Dances

Ballroom

Our classes cover all five dances of international style ballroom. Beginners start with Waltz and Quickstep, and as your experience and skills increase the other dances will start to look less impossible. For dancers of all levels we offer technique classes and private tuition from our professional coaches. If you're interested in dancing competitively, please see our Team page.

Waltz

One of the compulsory Beginners Team dances. The Waltz originated from Vienna and Austria in the 17th century. Throughout the 18th century a dance called the ‘waltzen’ developed. This dance was introduced at the beginning of the 19th century where it was met with some indignation as it was the first dance that was danced with the man’s hand around the lady’s waist. Fortunately the opposition to the dance dispersed allowing it to develop into what we now know as the Modern Waltz in its characteristic three-beat time.

Quickstep

This is the other compulsory Beginners Team ballroom. The Quickstep was originally a fast version of a Foxtrot, developed because many bands in the 1920s played the Foxtrot music too fast. The energy exerted during a 60 second Quickstep is the equivalent to that exerted by running a mile in record time. The Quickstep is the most popular ballroom dance today.

Foxtrot

The Foxtrot originated in 1913 from the Vaudeville act of actor Harry Fox. The dance contains a mixture of quick and slow steps and gives much more enjoyment than the one- and two-step that it replaced. It has smooth gliding steps that develop around the ballroom floor throughout the dance.

Tango

In the early 19th century the Tango was a solo dance performed by women. It originated from Argentina and spread through Europe in the 1900s. The phrasing is very important in Tango; there are major phrases, minor phrases, and Tango closes at the end of the phrase. There are many different types of Tango, however, the style that we dance is the International Tango.

Viennese Waltz

The Viennese Waltz is a much faster version of the Modern Waltz, but has the same origins. There are fewer steps to learn and they are especially versatile for covering the floor, but the speed more than enough makes up for the limited steps. The composers Franz Lanner and Johann Strauss from the 1830s are well noted for their beautiful Viennese Waltz pieces.

Latin

Our classes cover all five dances of international style latin. Beginners start with the well-known cha-cha and jive. We have medal classes from bronze level up to goldstar, technique classes and Beginners Team classes for those interested in dancing competitively in the university circuit at a national level. If you're interested in dancing competitively, please see our Team page.

Samba

While keeping elements of what the Brazilians believe is the true Samba, Ballroom Samba was formalized in 1956 by Pierre Lavelle and is what we dance in competitions today. It is made up of many different South American dances incorporated into one. It is very rhythmical with lots of hip action. There should be a good balance of moving steps and stationary steps and lots of outstretched arms. The accomplished dancer is made to look effortless and carefree with knee action, body sway, and "pendulum motion."

Jive

The most noticeable aspects of the Jive are its energy, speed, and bounce, it is one of the fastest dances and should show lots of kicks, flicks, and twirling of the lady. This rhythmical and swinging dance is influenced by the Boogie, Rock ‘n’ Roll, Swing, and Lindy Hop. This dance is usually performed last in competitions owing to the energy required to perform it.

Rumba

The Rumba originates from Cuba and it was first seen in the United States in 1920. It relies on the age-old premise of the lady trying to conquer the man by means of her womanly charms. Incorporating all the elements of teasing and withdrawal, it is considered the most sensual of the Latin dances. Look out for figure-eight hip rolls!

Paso Doble

The Paso Doble originated from southern France, although it is modeled after the drama and movement of a Spanish bullfight. This is the dance for the Man, he fill the "Space" with strong three-dimensional shapes and movements danced with "Pride and Dignity." The Paso is danced with a high chest and the shoulders wide and down, imitating the bullfight nature of the dance. It is almost always choreographed to ‘Spanish Gypsy Dance’ which has 3 highlights in the music, where dramatic positions are executed.

Cha-cha-cha

This is a cheeky, lively and flirtatious dance, it has a catch-me-if-you-can atmosphere, and is light and bubbly. By the early fifties it had developed from mambo into a dance in its own right and had been given the name ‘Chacha’. The name comes from the sound made by the feet during the 3-step change of weight, cha-cha-cha.

Casual Ballroom

"I really want to dance, but I don’t want to do competitions" you might think. The Ballroom and Latin Casual classes are exactly what you are looking for - it does not matter if you cannot commit to every single lesson or what your level is. These classes are for you to socialise with others, to enjoy the dance and the music and at the same time to learn what you want to learn. We offer Ballroom and Latin lessons for beginners as well as intermediate and advanced dancers.

For those who are completely new to dancing - do not worry about what to wear, just turn up to the lessons. But if you would like to dance more in the future, a pair of Ballroom or Latin dance shoes is a great investment!

If one day you decide that you want to see what competitions are like and give a try to the Team, simply talk to our team captains to understand the life of the competitive dancer.

Salsa

Need a break from studying? You should join our salsa classes! Whether you are a complete beginner or an advanced dancer, you can be sure that you'll find a class that is the right fit for your level. Our teacher Laith Sami is a salsa Europian Champion and is very active on the UK's salsa scene. Ask any of his students - he is a great performer, teacher and is also incredibly funny! Once that he has made you a salsa superstar, you will have many opportunities to show off your new skills on the dancefloor at one of our many socials. So leave those textbooks alone, put your coursework aside for a second and come have some fun with us!

As this is a social class, you can come along whenever you like and give salsa a try. You will get more out of the class if you attend every week though (and maybe practise in between) as this will allow you to progress to our more advanced salsa classes quickly! See the timetable for more info about when the classes are taking place.

Competitive Dancing

One of the main activities of IC Dance Club is competitive Ballroom and Latin dancing, also known as Dancesport. For those interested in going to competitions, we run classes at three levels: beginners, novice and intermediate/advanced. For beginners, we have the Beginners Team. For more experienced dancers, we have Novice and Int/Adv Technique practices. Finally, for those experienced dancers who want more practice and devote themselves to dancing as much as they can, we have the Main Team. The Beginners Team and the Main Team together form Imperial College Dance Team (ICDT or the Team for short), which represents Imperial in the various university competitions during the year!

Beginners Team

Are you a beginner and interested in dancing ballroom or latin competitively? Then join our Beginners Team, and be part of one of the most entertaining and rewarding adventures at Imperial!

The Beginners Team is specifically for absolute beginners who have little or no prior experience in Ballroom or Latin dancing. The classes start in October and there are two practices every week. You will begin by learning four dances - two ballroom (Waltz and Quickstep) and two latin (Cha-cha-cha and Jive).

After the first few weeks, you will be paired up with a partner and go to your first competition with the rest of the Team! We usually go to 4 university competitions in a year, where teams from different universities compete against each other. The beginners dance in their own category, so although it may seem scary at first, you will be competing against people who have the same experience as you. Competitions are one of the most entertaining and challenging parts of the Beginners Team - they give you a motivation to improve, as well as being amazingly fun team trips! (For more info, see the Competitions section).

As you progress through the year, you will be hearing more and more about the other six dances. After the last university competiton in the beginning of March, the beginners will start learning entirely new routines in those dances. This is a perfect time to join the Beginners Team if you have missed the beginning of the year and don't want to cut in the middle (which you are welcome to do if you wish to!). Otherwise to join at any time, simply turn up to one of the practices - you can find the schedule here.

Technique practices

We run four Technique practices every week, each one an hour long: Novice Ballroom, Novice Latin, Int/Adv Ballroom and Int/Adv Latin. The aim of these classes is to focus on the tricky bits of dancing and practice the technique of the steps.

In the Novice classes, you will spend time on Waltz, Quickstep, Cha-cha-cha and Jive, and you will work on improving aspects or steps that you often encounter in a routine, as for example holding your frame or spinning. You will also learn to perform steps from a third dance in each style that you might not be so familiar with (such as Tango or Paso Doble).

In Int/Adv, the steps are broken down much further, and more attention is paid on how to move different parts of your body and everything as a whole, how to connect with your partner, etc. The level of these classes is very high, so talk to the coach if you are unsure whether to go to Novice or Int/Adv.

During the Technique practices you will be thus learning technique that you can later apply to any choreography. They are very useful classes and your dancing will improve tremendously if you take even a little time to practise what you've learned before the next lesson.

Technique practices are also very suitable for people who really want to dance competitively, but do not yet have a partner. Apart from upkeeping and improving your dancing, they are meant to act as a platform for single dancers to meet other dancers of their level.

Main Team

The Main Team is composed of our most committed dancers above the beginner level. Main Team couples have two practices a week: one Main Team practice (one and a half hours on Wednesdays), and one Technique practice (one hour, usually on Saturdays).

Our Main Team includes dancers who have just graduated from the Beginners Team, all the way up to the most advanced couples - to be part of the Main Team, what matters is whether you want and are ready to commit to regular practice, rather than what your level is.

Being on the Main Team is a fantastic opportunity. For a start, we have coaches of an extremely high standard, who truly devote themselves to their students. In addition, belonging to a team that is practising hard towards a common goal is a very fulfilling experience... You will have seen each other so often and will have practised so hard together that, by the end of the season, it will start to feel something like... well, a team!

For more information about joining the Main Team, contact our team captains. The Main Team is priced differently from the rest of the classes - see the FAQ section for details.

Competitions

One of the most exciting aspects of being part of IC Dance Team is competing together as a team. During the first part of the year (September - March), the majority of the university competitions throughout the UK take place, and we traditionally attend four of them. This culminates with the Inter-Varsity Dance Competition (IVDC), held in the beginning of March in Blackpool - the biggest and most important university competition of the year.

In the second part of the competitive season (March - September), we attend open circuit competitions. These do not distinguish between students and ex-students as in the university circuit.

  • Sheffield Social (Sheffield, beginning of February)

FAQ

Do I need a partner?

For the larger part of our lessons - no! At all beginner classes you will get rotated in order to try out dancing with different people. As time goes on or if you decide to compete, you may of course settle down with one practice partner; there is however definitely no need to bring a partner with you to try out the classes - bring a friend along instead for some extra fun!!

If you are an experienced dancer looking for a partner, the best place to come to is one of the Technique practices - you will find other people of similar level to yours at these classes. However, to be part of the Main Team and attend the additional Main Team practice, you will need to have a partner, as only couples can join it.

How much does it cost?

Each class is charged by hour (except Main Team). One hour is £6 for non-members and £4 for members. Further discounts are available for those who want to commit to dance: if you are a member, you can get a bulk buy card of10 lessons for £35, which can be used for any of the weekly classes!

Those who wish to join the Main Team are required to purchase a Main Team pass for the whole term. The prices are set in the beginning of the year - for term 3 in 2014, it is £28 per person, per style: this includes the Wednesday Main Team practice and one hour Saturday technique practice for all the weeks in the term.

How do I get membership? (IC students)

Imperial College students and Union members can become members of IC Dance Club by purchasing membership online here. Once you've done that, bring a copy of your confirmation email to any class (on smartphone or otherwise), and we will give you a membership card which allows you to get 33% off all classes, and even more if you bulk buy! The membership is valid for the entire academic year and expires in the beginning of the next one in August; don't forget to always carry your card with you.

What if I am not a current IC student?

Other Further or Higher Education students, as well as Imperial ex-students and staff can also join and will need the following: Imperial College Union Membership AND IC Dance Club Membership. Instructions how to buy Imperial College Union Membership can be found here. Once you've bought Union Membership, you will need to buy Club Membership, which can be done online here. Note that without membership you will only be able to attend scheduled lessons and won't be able to use our practice space.

What's that buzz about free lessons?

To give you a chance to explore what's on offer, we run free taster sessions in October and we give you two free classes when purchasing your membership, as we know the best way to get you started is to get you dancing! The fastest way to hear about any deals we have is to subscribe to our mailing list or join our facebook groups - you can find links to them in the Contact section.
